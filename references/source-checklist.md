# Source Checklist

Use this list when the user wants source-backed output or when current facts matter.

## Public proof paths

### Company-side

- annual report;
- quarterly report;
- earnings call;
- investor presentation;
- exchange filing;
- capital raise documents;
- major customer concentration disclosures.

### Industry-side

- standards groups;
- trade conferences;
- technical presentations;
- customer qualification references;
- procurement and project filings;
- environmental or capacity approvals;
- customs or shipment hints where legally and practically available.

### Market-side

- analyst coverage only as support, not proof;
- specialist trade press for context;
- government or defense documents when the theme is policy-driven.

## Verification prompts

Ask:

- Which customer actually depends on this layer?
- Is the company named, implied, or only guessed?
- Is the bottleneck technical, operational, or temporary?
- Is there evidence of qualification or production, not just sampling?
- Does financial disclosure show the story is starting to matter?

## Red flags

- "leader in AI" with no chain-specific evidence;
- only social proof and no primary-source follow-through;
- tiny relevant revenue inside a large unrelated business;
- thesis depends on one speculative customer win;
- production timing pushed beyond the market window;
- debt or dilution more urgent than the story.
